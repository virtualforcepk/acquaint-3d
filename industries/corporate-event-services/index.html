Why it matters

The RFP is often lost before the call.

A corporate marketing manager or event planner building a shortlist for a trade-show build or a conference production doesn't call every vendor — they cut the list first, using whatever they can find online in ten minutes. A dated site, unclear past work, or no obvious way to start a conversation gets you quietly dropped, no explanation given. The firms that stay in the room are the ones whose site does the pre-qualifying work for them: clear capabilities, real scope, credible presence, an obvious next step. That's not decoration — it's the first stage of the RFP, whether anyone calls it that or not.

The leak most firms don't see

Booth traffic and inbound that never gets followed up.

Trade-show and exhibit companies live on two lead sources: the corporate stakeholders they need in front of them on the show floor, and the inbound inquiries that land on their site afterward from planners and marketing managers researching vendors. Most of that inbound leaks — a contact form nobody checks daily, no clear next step, no follow-up system. We build the site and the capture flow so that inbound gets answered fast and tracked properly, instead of sitting in an inbox until it goes cold.
